About Me

Hello, I'm Syl Shaw

I am a second-year PhD student in Theoretical Condensed Matter Physics at the University of Warwick. My research focuses on topological matter, quantum-Hall effects and phase transitions.

My recent work involved calculating the localisation critical exponent of the quantum Hall transition using a real-space renormalisation group approach, and I am currently working on real-space approaches to topological matter.

Current Research

Quantum Hall Transition

Investigating the critical exponent of the quantum Hall transition using a real-space renormalisation approach to the Chalker-Coddington model and extensions thereof.

Topology of Lieb lattices

Understanding how 2D and 3D Lieb lattices can host novel topological states. We examine the topology in real-space both with and without disorder.
